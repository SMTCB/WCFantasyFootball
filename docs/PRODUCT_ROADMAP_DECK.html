Our Methodology

Human-Centered Product Evolution

We apply IDEO design thinking to a four-phase evolution. Each phase ships a complete, valuable product on its own — while quietly laying the rails for the next.

🔍
Empathize
Understand fantasy sports fans across sports, geographies, and engagement levels. What keeps them coming back week after week?
💡
Define
The core problem is fragmentation — fans use a different app per sport. We unify the experience under one brand.
🎨
Ideate
Explore platform models: single-sport mastery → multi-sport hub → white-label infrastructure → real sports ecosystem.
🛠️
Prototype
Test each phase with real users. Run multi-sport in beta during Phase 2 before pursuing enterprise customers in Phase 3.
📊
Test & Measure
Validate revenue models and retention. Data — not opinion — informs every phase-gate decision.
Guiding Principle
Each phase is a complete, valuable product on its own. We don't force users into Phase N+1 features — we delight them with Phase N experiences while building toward what's next.
Phase 1 · 2026 · Current

Single-Sport Mastery

Establish Forza Fantasy League as the premier Premier League fantasy football app. Build a loyal, engaged user base with exceptional UX — and lay the data infrastructure for everything that comes next.

Functional Goals

  • 11-player squad builder with formation validation
  • Real-time league standings & H2H scoring
  • Live match updates with Joker chip
  • League chat & community features
  • Mobile-native iOS / Android (Capacitor)
  • Cross-platform sync (web + mobile)

Business Goals

  • 50K active users (year-end target)
  • $2–5/user/month on premium tiers
  • 40%+ retention after onboarding
  • Launch seasonal leagues (auto-refresh)
  • Establish brand in UK & Europe
  • Prove product-market fit
Why This Phase
Perfect the core fantasy football experience. Build a brand known for quality. Establish the data infrastructure and behavioural insights that make multi-sport expansion possible without re-platforming.

Strategic Reality Check

Risks & Opportunities

The honest picture: what could break the plan, and what could break it open.

🔴 Critical Risks

Regulatory Risk
Gambling regulations vary by jurisdiction. College sports especially fraught (NCAA, state-by-state).
API / Data Risk
Heavy dependence on official sports APIs. Provider cuts access → product breaks.
Competitive Pressure
ESPN, DraftKings, FanDuel could build similar white-label, with bigger balance sheets.
Multi-Sport Retention
Harder to retain casual users across 10 sports than 1. Engagement fragments.

🟢 Key Opportunities

International Expansion
Cricket (India, Pakistan), La Liga (Spain), EPL (UK/global) = 3B+ addressable market.
Esports Dominance
No unified fantasy esports platform yet. First-mover advantage is real.
Inbound White-Label Demand
LaLiga, NBA actively seeking partners. The "build vs buy" lean is shifting to buy.
Betting Partner Moats
Exclusive affiliate deals with DraftKings/FanDuel can lock competitors out of channels.
Mitigation Strategy
Regulatory: Partner with local legal counsel. Start in regulated geographies (UK, NJ, NY). · Data: Build redundant API relationships early; consider custom integrations where needed. · Competition: Move fast to white-label (2028). Lock in league partnerships before larger players notice. · Retention: Design for casual users — sport-specific communities matter; not all users need all sports.
