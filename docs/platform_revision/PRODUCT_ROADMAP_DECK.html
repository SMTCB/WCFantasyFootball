State of play · 2026-05-16

Where we are now

Phase 1 has shipped most of what it needs. The product works. The next milestone is users, retention, and revenue — not more features.

What's working

  • EPL fantasy live on Vercel (web + Capacitor mobile shell)
  • 37 core features shipped: squads, transfers, leagues, chat, scoring, betting widget, draft, auctions
  • 178/178 E2E tests passing on CI (Node 24)
  • Scoring pipeline (Edge Functions) ingests Forza data and computes points reliably
  • Multi-competition database schema work already underway (PR #66)

What's not

  • iOS / Android apps not yet in stores — must ship before EPL 2026/27 season opens (mid-August)
  • No premium tier, no ads, no affiliate revenue. Revenue today: £0
  • MAU / retention numbers unknown to this assessment — must instrument with PostHog / Mixpanel in Q2 2026
  • Forza is sole data provider; contract terms need negotiation
  • No backup data source contingency
What Phase 1 "complete" actually means
Five hard gates: ≥50K MAU, ≥35% Day-30 retention, ≥2.5% premium take-rate, ≥£0.40 ARPU/MAU/month, and apps live in both stores at ≥4.3 rating. All five — not three of five.

The takeaway
The fantasy market we can address is real but bounded. The B2B fantasy infrastructure space — turnkey modern fantasy UX for tier-2 broadcasters — is fragmented and under-served. That is the actual blue ocean, not "5 sports."

The wedge in B2B
None of the incumbents lead with turnkey modern fantasy UX. Tier-2 broadcasters (DAZN, BT Sport, beIN, Premier Sports, FloSports, Now Sports) either build in-house for 24 months or license data and still build UI themselves. We offer option C: licensed product, modern UX, customer brings data rights.
Positioning

Where Forza wins

Three structural advantages that compound. Anything outside these is a bet against the strategy.

⚡
Modern social UX
Real-time chat with @mentions, edit/delete, search. Push notifications. Mobile-native. League-as-community, not league-as-spreadsheet. FPL Official structurally cannot ship these in 12 months.
⚽
Cross-competition leagues
One league mixing EPL, UCL, and La Liga players with normalised scoring. No incumbent offers this — FPL Official is locked to one comp by IP; UCL Fantasy is locked to UCL.
🏗️
Modern stack as B2B asset
Supabase + React + Capacitor + multi-tenant ready. A broadcaster can deploy us in 6 months, not 24. Tier-2 broadcasters cannot afford in-house teams at modern UX bar.
What we are NOT optimising for
  • Beating FPL Official on scale or brand. We won't; they have a league licence and free price.
  • Beating DraftKings on betting. We don't operate; we affiliate.
  • Beating Dream11 in India. Don't enter. Different regulatory regime, different game.
  • Owning NBA / NFL / MLB fantasy. ESPN, Sleeper, Yahoo, and DraftKings dominate. We do not extend there.

Strategy: football platform, not multi-sport
Top 4 sports by score are all football competitions. They reuse 95%+ of our engine and address the same user. Five-sport multi-sport was rejected because it would have meant 5 new scoring engines, 5 new data integrations, and 5 user bases with low overlap to EPL.
Phase 1 · 2026 · Current

EPL Mastery

Win the EPL fantasy product on UX. Reach 50K MAU and prove premium take-rate works. Don't add scope.

In scope

  • EPL 2026/27 season launch (15 Aug 2026)
  • iOS + Android live in stores by 15 July
  • Premium subscription tier (£3.99/mo, single tier)
  • Push notifications (transfer deadlines, gameweek live, mentions)
  • Refer-a-friend mechanic
  • Editorial / content layer (1 article / week)
  • Trade system + cup mode (existing backlog)

Out of scope (deliberately)

  • Any second sport or competition
  • Any white-label tooling
  • Real-money betting integration (affiliate widget only)
  • Internationalisation beyond English
  • Any "moonshot" ML features
Gate to Phase 2 — all five must pass
  • ≥50K MAU during EPL season
  • ≥35% Day-30 retention on August launch cohort
  • ≥2.5% premium take-rate
  • ≥£0.40 ARPU per MAU per month (blended)
  • Apps live in both stores at ≥4.3 rating with ≥250 reviews each
Phase 2 · Q4 2026 → Q3 2027

Football Platform

Add Champions League, then La Liga, then optionally Bundesliga or Serie A. Launch cross-competition leagues — the differentiator no incumbent offers. Sign the first white-label pilot.

Why football, not multi-sport

  • Reuses 95%+ of existing scoring engine and schema
  • Same data provider (Forza covers all top European football)
  • Same user base — EPL → UCL transition rate ~4× cross-sport rate
  • Defensible positioning: "modern football platform" is a real category
  • Lower technical risk; lower data cost

What ships

  • UCL knockouts fantasy (private beta Q4 26, public Feb 27)
  • La Liga fantasy (Q1 27)
  • Cross-competition leagues — THE differentiator
  • Multi-tenant DB foundations (Q4 26)
  • Federated auth / SSO for white-label tenants
  • Configurable scoring admin UI

Phase 3 reality check
Phase 3 needs 3–6 active customers to hit £800K–£2.5M ARR from B2B. That's a 12–18 month sales cycle each. First customer in Q4 2027 → second mid-2028 → third late 2028. Conservative. Anyone promising "5–10 enterprise customers by 2029" is selling a pitch deck, not a plan.
Phase 4 · 2029+

Selective Ecosystem

By 2029 we'll have data we cannot predict today. This phase is intentionally vague. What we know now: it's selective, not expansionist.

On the table

  • Cricket B2B with a non-Indian cricket board (West Indies, Cricket Australia, PCB) — if WL customer pays for it
  • Esports single-tournament product (e.g., LoL Worlds) — brand extension
  • US fantasy entry with geo-blocking (excluding restricted states)
  • Australia / Republic of Ireland consumer launch
  • Affiliate revenue scaled to £1M+ via partner network

Off the table

  • NFL / NBA / MLB consumer — too dominated, too different
  • India consumer — Dream11 + GST regime
  • Becoming a licensed sportsbook — regulatory burden, brand risk
  • Crypto / NFT mechanics — Sorare already lost that bet
  • Generative AI features as a wedge — short half-life
Phase 4 strategic option
At ~£3M ARR with growing B2B mix, the company is a credible strategic acquisition target for STATS Perform, Genius, Sportradar, or a media/broadcast group. That optionality is created in Phase 2/3 — Phase 4 is when it crystallises.
