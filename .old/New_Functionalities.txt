## 01 — PREDICTED LINEUPS & INJURY ALERTS
 
```
# FantasyKit — Predicted Lineups & Injury Alerts
## Antigravity Build Prompt
 
You are building a pre-match intelligence layer that shows users which players are likely to start, and which are injury doubts, before squad lock.
 
---
 
### PREDICTED LINEUPS
 
WHAT IT IS
24–48 hours before kick-off, show AI-predicted starting XI for each team playing that matchday. Sourced from the API-Football lineup prediction endpoint. This is one of the highest-value pieces of information in fantasy football — users currently leave your app to check Forza or ESPN for this.
 
WHERE IT APPEARS
1. In the player search screen — each player card shows a small badge: 🟢 "likely starter" (confidence > 75%) or 🟡 "likely bench" (confidence < 50%) or empty (50-75% confidence)
2. In the squad builder pitch view — owned players who are injury doubts or bench predictions get a visual warning (amber border around the player card on the pitch)
3. On the home screen, above the match list — for each match with predicted lineups available: "Starting lineups predicted for BRA vs KOR — 98 players confirmed"
 
DESIGN
- Badge placement: top-right of player card in search / player selector
- Badge style: 11px text, emoji + label, pill shape with 100px border-radius
- Confidence levels: >75% = green bg + white text ("likely starter"), 50-75% = grey (neutral), <50% = amber + dark text ("likely bench")
- Squad builder warning: amber left border (2px) on the player card if <60% confidence
- Pitch view note: "Updated 24h ago · API-Football" in 10px muted grey at the bottom of the predicted lineups banner
 
DATA MODEL
Prediction: id, fixture_id, team_id, player_id, position, confidence_score (0-100), updated_at
 
API INTEGRATION
Call API-Football lineup prediction endpoint 36 hours before each match. Cache predictions in Supabase. Update once daily. If predictions aren't available for a match, hide all badges for that match and show a neutral state.
 
---
 
### INJURY ALERTS
 
WHAT IT IS
Real-time player status updates — injuries, suspensions, doubts, and returns. Sourced from API-Football player injuries endpoint. Displayed as visual warnings in the squad builder and search screens.
 
INJURY STATES (use these exact labels)
- 🟢 Fit — player available
- 🟡 Doubt — player is training but not confirmed fit (50/50)
- 🔴 Out — ruled out for this matchday (injury or suspension)
- 🟠 Returning — expected back next matchday (monitor them)
 
WHERE INJURY STATUS APPEARS
1. In the player search screen — injury badge shown next to the player name (red "OUT" pill if unavailable)
2. In the squad builder — "DANGER ZONE" collapsible section at the top listing all squad members with injury status != fit. Shows: player name + status + returning matchday (if applicable)
3. In the squad builder player card (on pitch) — red "INJURED" badge if status = out
4. On the home screen, in a notification banner — "⚠️ 3 players in your squad are injury doubts (tap to review)" — appears 24h before squad lock if any owned players are doubts
 
DESIGN
- Injury badge: 11px, pill-shaped, emoji + status label, high contrast (red for OUT, amber for DOUBT)
- Danger Zone section: expands from the top of the squad builder, light red background (#FCEBEB or similar), 1px left border red, shows all at-risk players in a sortable list (sort by: likely to play first, then by importance to your squad)
- Push notification: sent 24h before lock if any squad member is a doubt or out — "⚠️ [Player] is an injury doubt. Confirm or transfer."
- Transfer CTA: "Transfer out now" button in the Danger Zone section — tapping opens the player swap flow with suggestions for replacements at the same position + same price range
 
DATA MODEL
PlayerStatus: id, player_id, fixture_id, status (fit|doubt|out|returning), return_date (null if not applicable), last_updated_at
 
API INTEGRATION
Call API-Football injuries endpoint daily (ideally every 8 hours during match week). Store in Supabase. When a player's status changes, trigger a push notification to users who own them in active leagues.
 
LOGIC RULES
- If a player is marked OUT, they cannot be selected in the squad builder (grayed out in the picker)
- If a player is DOUBT, allow selection but show the amber warning
- Update the "DANGER ZONE" section in real-time if the user is viewing the squad builder while injury news breaks
- When squad locks, if an OUT player is still in the squad, the system auto-transfers them out to the highest-rated available replacement at the same position (and notifies the user: "Your transfer was auto-filled: [Player] → [Replacement]")
 
---
 
### MOCK DATA TO BUILD WITH

Predicted lineups (API-Football response):
- Vinicius Jr. (BRA, LW) · confidence 89% → 🟢 likely starter
- Mbappé (FRA, FW) · confidence 78% → 🟢 likely starter
- Bellingham (ENG, MF) · confidence 68% → neutral
- Pedri (ESP, MF) · confidence 52% → neutral
- Salah (EGY, FW) · confidence 42% → 🟡 likely bench
 
Injuries (as of 24h before lock):
- Neymar (BRA, LW) · status: OUT (ankle) · return: Matchday 5
- De Bruyne (BEL, MF) · status: DOUBT · fitness: 60%
- Hakimi (MAR, DF) · status: FIT
 
Squad builder with Danger Zone:

1. De Bruyne (MF) — DOUBT (60% fit) — you own · Transfer options: [Bellingham] [Pedri]
2. (optional) Neymar (LW) — OUT until MD5 — you own · Transfer out immediately
 
---

Build the Danger Zone section first — it's the most-used feature. Then add the predicted lineup badges to the player search screen. Finally, integrate the auto-transfer logic for OUT players at squad lock.
 
The injury alerts are the foundation that makes the predicted lineups feel real — they work together to create a complete pre-match intelligence layer.

After each matchday finishes (all matches complete), every user receives a personalized recap card that appears on their home screen. The card summarizes: points scored, rank movement, best player, captain performance, and league position.
 
The card is shareable — it's also a static image (generated server-side or client-side via canvas) that users can send to their league group chat or social media.

Option 2 (server-side, scalable):

For PoC: use client-side. Later, add server-side rendering if you want to cache images or add dynamic elements.

Generate the recap 10 minutes after the last fixture of the matchday ends.

- Captain: query captain_id and captain_points
- Rank change: compare previous matchday rank to current rank
- Image generation: trigger on creation OR on first share (lazy generation)
 
The image should be cached (stored in Supabase Storage or similar) after first generation. Include an expiry — reuse the same image for 30 days, then regenerate if the user reshares.

## 03 — LIVE PROJECTIONS
 
```
# FantasyKit — Live Projections
## Antigravity Build Prompt
 
You are building a live projection system that shows each user's projected final score during a live matchday, updating every few minutes as players accumulate points.
 
---
 
### WHAT IT IS
 
During a live match, show a secondary projected score for each user: their current live points + an estimated additional points based on remaining time and each player's average points-per-90 rate.
 
Example: "You: 74 pts live → projected 91 pts at FT"
 
The projection updates every 5 minutes, changes colour (green if projected to go up, red if going down from the previous projection), and sits directly below the live score on the leaderboard and the user's home screen card.
 
### WHERE PROJECTIONS APPEAR
 
1. **Leaderboard during live matches** — under each user's live score in grey text (slightly smaller font)
2. **Home screen match card** — below the live score: "Live: 74 pts → Projected: 91 pts at FT"
3. **Your live feed** — a sticky card at the top showing your projection: "Your score: 74 live | Projected finish: 91 pts (↑ from 88)"
4. **Match detail screen** — all players on the pitch with remaining minutes, shown as a % progress bar
 
### PROJECTION FORMULA
 
For each user's squad:
 
```
projected_total = current_points + sum(projected_remaining_points)
 
projected_remaining_points = 
  for each player still on pitch (minutes_played < 90):
    minutes_remaining = 90 - minutes_played
    minutes_remaining_normalized = minutes_remaining / 90
    expected_points_per_90 = (player_season_avg_points_per_match) OR (position_avg) if no season data
    
    projected_points = expected_points_per_90 * minutes_remaining_normalized
 
example:
- Vinicius Jr. (80' played, 45 pts in season, 8 matches = 5.6 pts/match)
- minutes_remaining = 10, normalized = 0.11
- expected = 5.6 * 0.11 = 0.62 pts → round to 0.5 or 1 pt
- final estimate: current_score + 0.5
```
 
The formula is simple and conservative on purpose — it should never be more than ±3 pts off actual final. If data is missing, default to position average: GK 2.1 pts/90, DF 2.8, MF 3.2, FW 4.1.
 
### DESIGN
 
- Projected score text: same size as live score but 70% opacity, grey (#999999)
- Projection icon: small upward/downward arrow emoji next to the projected number (green ↑ or red ↓)
- Change indicator: "↑ from 88" or "↓ from 92" in 10px, appears on the sticky card if the projection went up/down since the last update (5 min ago)
- Update frequency: every 5 minutes during active matches, live score updates happen as events occur (goals, cards) and projection updates follow within 30 seconds
- Leaderboard: projection colour shifts based on direction: green-tinted if projected up this cycle, red-tinted if down, neutral if flat
 
### DATA & REFRESH
 
Projection is calculated server-side (not client-side) to ensure consistency.
 
Refresh logic:
- Every 5 minutes, a Cloudflare Worker queries the live match state (current minute, current points by user)
- Calculates projections using the formula above
- Broadcasts updated projections via Supabase Realtime to all league members viewing that match
- When a scoring event happens (goal, card, sub), recalculate immediately for affected players
 
Store projections in a temporary table (ProjectionSnapshot) to track history — this helps you validate the formula accuracy and shows users their projection history as a sparkline later (if someone wants to see how their projection moved during the match).
 
### PSYCHOLOGICAL RULES
 
This feature is incredibly engaging but can cause anxiety if not handled carefully:
 
1. Never let a projection go negative — if remaining minutes can't realistically add points, show the current score, not a projection
2. Show confidence: if a user's projection hasn't changed in 10 minutes, add a "stable" indicator
3. Be conservative: better to undershoot and surprise users with a higher final score than overshoot and disappoint
4. Dampen extreme swings: if a projection jumps by >5 pts in one cycle, smooth it (show the change over 2-3 updates instead of 1)
 
### MOCK MATCH STATE TO BUILD WITH
 
Match: BRA 2–0 KOR · Minute 72
 
User: João (team: 3 players on pitch)
- Vinicius Jr. (LW, 72' played, 15 pts scored so far) → 18 remaining minutes
  - season avg: 5.6 pts/match
  - projected remaining: 5.6 * (18/90) = 1.1 pts → 1 pt
  
- Bellingham (MF, 72' played, 8 pts scored) → 18 remaining
  - season avg: 3.8 pts/match
  - projected remaining: 3.8 * (18/90) = 0.76 → 0.5 pt
  
- Ederson (GK, 72' played, 4 pts + clean sheet 4 pts = 8 pts) → 18 remaining
  - season avg (GK): 2.1 pts/match
  - projected remaining: 2.1 * (18/90) = 0.42 → 0 pt
  - clean sheet still intact so assume it holds → +4 pts likely
 
Current live score: 74 pts
Projected remaining: 1 + 0.5 + 4 = 5.5 → round to 5 pts
**Projected final: 79 pts**
 
Display:
"Live: 74 pts → Projected: 79 pts ↑" (if it was 78 last cycle, show ↑)
 
---
 
### START HERE
 
Build the projection card on the home screen first (mock data). Then integrate the real formula with the live match state. Finally, add the leaderboard projection column and the sticky card on the live feed.
 
Test the projection accuracy against historical matches — measure how often the final projection is within ±2 pts of actual final score. Iterate the formula if needed (e.g. adjust GK/DF/MF/FW position averages).
 
This feature is ESPN's single most-cited engagement tool — users watch the projection ticker as much as they watch the match. The implementation looks simple but the psychological impact is enormous.

## 04 — VAR HANDLING
 
```
# FantasyKit — VAR (Video Assistant Referee) Handling
## Antigravity Build Prompt
 
You are building a robust VAR event system that handles the emotional and scoring complexity of VAR decisions. This is critical because VAR can award or reverse points, and if handled poorly, it creates massive user frustration.
 
---
 
### THE PROBLEM VAR SOLVES
 
In fantasy football, when VAR reviews a goal, the points are awarded before the decision is final. If VAR reverses a goal, the points disappear. This is confusing and frustrating if not handled explicitly. Users need to know: did the goal stand, is it under review, or was it cancelled?
 
### VAR STATES
 
Every goal has these possible states:
 
1. **GOAL AWARDED** — goal is confirmed, points awarded (normal flow)
2. **UNDER REVIEW** — goal is under VAR review, points temporarily shown but marked as pending, not locked
3. **GOAL STANDS** — VAR confirmed the goal, points are locked in
4. **GOAL REVERSED** — VAR cancelled the goal, points are removed, user sees a reversal notification
 
### VISUAL DESIGN FOR EACH STATE
 
**GOAL AWARDED (before VAR check, if applicable)**
- Event feed item: ⚽ Player name scored 45' — points +5 shown in green
- Leaderboard: user's score updates immediately
- Status: no badge, assume it will stand unless VAR is reviewing
 
**UNDER REVIEW**
- Event feed item: ⚽ Player name scored 45' — points +5 shown in yellow, with a small badge: "📺 UNDER REVIEW"
- Leaderboard: user's score shows the provisional points but in 50% opacity, with a small icon next to it: 📺
- Live feed sticky card: "Your score: 74 (provisional, +1 under review)" — the under-review points are coloured yellow
- Countdown timer: if the review takes >60 seconds, show "Review in progress — estimated 30s more"
 
**GOAL STANDS**
- Event feed item: the yellow badge changes to green: ✅ CONFIRMED
- Leaderboard: score fully opaque, points locked in
- Notification: brief toast at top of screen: "✅ Vinicius goal confirmed — +5 pts locked in"
 
**GOAL REVERSED**
- Event feed item: shows a strikethrough version of the goal: ~~⚽ Vinicius scored~~ — points crossed out
- New item appears right below: ❌ Goal cancelled by VAR — points reversed
- Leaderboard: score decreases, shows a red "−5" badge briefly
- Notification: prominent red banner at top: "⚠️ Vinicius goal cancelled by VAR — you lost 5 pts (was 74, now 69)"
- User's rank may drop — show this in real-time with an animated downward rank number
 
### RANKING IMPACT DURING VAR
 
If a goal reversal causes a rank change (e.g. you drop from 2nd to 3rd), show this immediately:
- Rank number animates red for 2 seconds
- Downward arrow appears (↓)
- The activity wall registers the rank change: "📉 You dropped 1 place (goal cancelled)"
 
### CRITICAL RULES
 
1. **Never silently remove points.** Always create an explicit event that shows the removal.
2. **Provisional points are always visually distinct** from locked points. Use opacity, badges, or colour.
3. **Reversals always trigger a notification.** Loud red banner at the top, not a subtle event feed item.
4. **Show the context.** When points are reversed, tell the user why: "Goal reversed — offside detected by VAR" or "Goal reversed — handball in buildup."
5. **Timestamp VAR decisions.** Show how long the review took: "Review took 2m 14s" — adds legitimacy.
 
### API INTEGRATION
 
API-Football returns VAR events in the match event feed with `type: "var"` and `detail: "goal_cancelled"` or `detail: "goal_confirmed"`.
 
Flow:
1. Goal event comes in (type: goal) → mark as GOAL AWARDED, award points
2. VAR event comes in with goal_confirmed detail → change state to GOAL STANDS, badge updates
3. VAR event comes in with goal_cancelled detail → change state to GOAL REVERSED, remove points, fire notification
 
### DATA MODEL
 
Goal: id, fixture_id, player_id, minute, type, var_status (none|under_review|confirmed|reversed), var_decision_minute (nullable), var_reason (nullable)
 
When var_status changes, trigger:
- Event log entry (for the activity wall)
- Points recalculation for affected squads
- Push notification to affected users
- Rank recalculation and broadcast
 
### MOCK VAR SEQUENCE TO BUILD WITH
 
Minute 45: Vinicius Jr. scores for BRA
- Event: ⚽ Goal awarded, +5 pts to João
- João's rank: 2nd, 74 pts
- Activity wall: "⚽ Vinicius scored BRA · João +5 pts"
 
Minute 45+30s: VAR review starts
- Event: 📺 UNDER REVIEW badge appears on goal
- João's event: goal badge shows yellow "📺 UNDER REVIEW"
- João's score: still shows 74 but in reduced opacity
- Activity wall: "📺 Goal under review — Vinicius / BRA (expected 30s)"
 
Minute 46+15s: VAR confirms
- Event: ✅ GOAL STANDS
- João's score: back to full opacity, +5 locked in
- Notification: "✅ Vinicius goal confirmed — +5 pts locked"
- Activity wall: yellow badge becomes green ✅
 
Alternative scenario (minute 46+30s): VAR cancels
- Event: ❌ GOAL REVERSED — offside
- João's score: 74 → 69 (red animation)
- João's rank: 2nd → 3rd (red downward animation)
- Prominent notification: "⚠️ Vinicius goal cancelled — offside. You lost 5 pts. Now 3rd."
- Activity wall: new event appears: "❌ Vinicius goal reversed (offside) · João −5 pts"
 
---
 
### START HERE
 
Build the UNDER REVIEW → GOAL STANDS flow first. This is the happy path and the most common case. Then add the GOAL REVERSED flow with the notification logic.
 
Test with a recorded match that had a VAR incident — pull the real event data from API-Football and walk through the state changes to ensure they're clear and unambiguous.
 
VAR handling is not glamorous, but getting it wrong is one of the fastest ways to lose user trust. A user who watches +5 pts become −5 pts without understanding why will disable your app's notifications and never play again.

## 05 — HEAD-TO-HEAD RECORD
 
```
# FantasyKit — Head-to-Head Record
## Antigravity Build Prompt
 
You are building a persistent H2H record view that tracks every matchday between two users across an entire tournament, creating a personal rivalry narrative.
 
---
 
### WHAT IT IS
 
A H2H record lives inside the league and shows the matchday-by-matchday history between you and any rival: wins, draws, losses, points for/against, biggest win, current streak, and a detailed matchday table.
 
Think of it as the league memory — it turns abstract leaderboard positions into personal stories: "I'm 4–2 against Ricardo this season."
 
### WHERE IT APPEARS
 
1. **From the leaderboard** — tap any rival's name/rank → opens their H2H card (bottom sheet)
2. **From the activity wall** — tap any call-out card or rival event → shows the H2H record with that user
3. **League stats section** — a "Rivalries" tab showing your record against each opponent (sortable by win/loss/streak)
 
### H2H CARD ANATOMY
 
[ Header: your avatar + name vs. rival's avatar + name ]
[ Overall record: "4–2–1" (wins–losses–draws) · total matchdays played ]
[ Stat boxes: Points for (320) · Points against (298) · Biggest win (+45 pts) · Current streak (2 wins) ]
[ Detailed table: matchday-by-matchday breakdown (see below) ]
[ Timeline: sparkline showing your points (blue) vs. their points (red) across all matchdays ]
 
### DETAILED MATCHDAY TABLE
 
For each matchday you both played in (in reverse chronological order):
 
| Matchday | You | Rival | Outcome | Your top player | Their top player |
|----------|-----|-------|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 4 | 74 pts (rank 2) | 87 pts (rank 1) | Loss | Vinicius 15 pts | Mbappé 18 pts |
| 3 | 92 pts (rank 1) | 68 pts (rank 4) | Win | Bellingham 12 pts | De Bruyne 9 pts |
| 2 | 71 pts (rank 3) | 71 pts (rank 3) | Draw | Salah 10 pts | Pedri 10 pts |
 
Each row is tappable → expands to show full squad matchup (your squad vs. their squad, position by position, comparing points)
 
### STAT CALCULATIONS
 
H2H Record:
- Wins: count matchdays where your_points > rival_points
- Losses: count matchdays where your_points < rival_points
- Draws: count matchdays where your_points == rival_points
- Head-to-head matchdays: sum of all three
 
Points for/against:
- Points for: sum of your points across all H2H matchdays
- Points against: sum of rival's points across all H2H matchdays
- Average per matchday: points for / matchdays
 
Biggest win: max(your_points − rival_points) in a single matchday
Biggest loss: max(rival_points − your_points) in a single matchday (shown as a negative stat)
 
Current streak:
- Sequence of wins/losses from most recent matchday backwards
- Display as: "2 wins in a row" or "Lost last 3" — if streak is ≥3, highlight it in the card header
 
### DATA MODEL
 
HeadToHeadRecord: id, league_id, user_a_id, user_b_id, matchday_id, user_a_points, user_b_points, winner (a|b|draw)
 
Derived stats (cached and recalculated after each matchday):
- wins_a, losses_a, draws_a
- points_for_a, points_against_a
- biggest_win_a, biggest_loss_a
- current_streak_a (calculated from latest matchdays)
 
### SPARKLINE VISUALIZATION
 
On the H2H card, show a mini sparkline (100px × 40px) with:
- X-axis: matchdays (MD1 to MD4)
- Y-axis: points (0 to max of the two users' highest scores)
- Blue line: your points
- Red line: rival's points
- Intersections marked with a dot
- Hover/tap a point → shows the matchday number and the exact points
 
Simple implementation: use a lightweight charting library (Recharts) or SVG path.
 
### STREAK INDICATORS
 
If you're on a winning streak ≥2, add a small badge: "🔥 2 wins"
If you're on a losing streak ≥2, show: "📉 Lost 2 in a row"
If you've never beaten this opponent: "0–N record" in a neutral grey
If you've never lost to them: "N–0 record" in a bold green
 
### ACTIVITY WALL INTEGRATION
 
When a matchday ends and ranks are calculated, the activity wall can optionally generate H2H events for the biggest rivals:
 
"🏆 You beat Ricardo 81–72 — your record: 3–2 against him"
"⚠️ You lost to João 87–74 — record: 2–4, he's on a 2-game streak"
 
These are auto-generated only if it's a rival you've played at least 2 matchdays against (to avoid noise for one-off matchups).
 
---

Matchdays:
