// The Opportunity

WHY NOW.
WHY 2026.

World Cup 2026 is the largest sporting event in history by viewership. Fantasy sports globally are growing at 12% CAGR. The official fantasy product has well-documented UX failures. The gap is real, the timing is perfect, and no credible alternative exists yet.

🌍

Global Market

Fantasy sports: $27B in 2024, $48–55B by 2030. Football-specific fantasy is the fastest-growing segment globally — led by Europe and South Asia.

FPL: 11M managers/season

⚽

World Cup Specific

FIFA World Cup Fantasy 2022 drew ~3.7M users — a severe undercount. The official product launched late, had weak UX, no social features, and poor mobile performance.

2026: 48 teams, 104 matches, 6B viewers

🎯

The Addressable Gap

0.5% of a 20M-user World Cup fantasy audience = 100,000 registered users. 1% = 200,000. Monetisable subset (willing to pay) historically runs 8–15% of free users.

Conservative target: 75K users

🏆 The North American Effect

The 2026 host-nation effect drives a genuine surge in U.S. and Canadian soccer fandom. These users are fantasy-literate (NFL/NBA culture) but soccer-fantasy novices. ForzaKit can be the onboarding product for this cohort — while still satisfying the FPL veteran with its depth. No competitor is positioned to serve both audiences simultaneously.

// Competitive Landscape

THE GAP
NOBODY FILLS

Every competitor has a clear weakness. ForzaKit's four-way combination — chip depth + social leagues + daily engagement hook + bracket view — is the defensible surface area no one currently owns.

🏛️

FIFA Official Fantasy

Brand authority, official data. But: historically late to launch, uninspired UX, no social layer, no chip depth, poor mobile. Sets the floor of user expectations — not the ceiling.

ForzaKit: "The fan product. Not the official one."

📊

Fantasy Premier League

10M+ users, gold-standard chip mechanics, but structurally incapable of serving the World Cup. FPL is a marathon. ForzaKit is for sprinters.

Best recruitment pool: FPL users who play every year

💎

Sorare

Crypto-native, card-collector audience. Market contracted post-NFT peak. High onboarding friction, presupposes crypto literacy. Different psychographic entirely.

ForzaKit: "Zero barriers. Knowledge is the currency."

🎲

DraftKings / FanDuel

Gambling-adjacent, transactional, antisocial — you play against the house, not your friends. U.S.-centric regulation. DFS is for the grinder; ForzaKit is for the group chat.

ForzaKit: "Globally accessible, non-monetary stakes."

✦ The Four-Way Moat

No competitor currently offers: (1) FPL-quality chip mechanics adapted to a World Cup format, (2) social private leagues with in-app chat, (3) a daily engagement hook (Daily Joker), and (4) a bracket view combining tournament progression with fantasy standing. This combination is uniquely ForzaKit's.

// Revenue Model

FIVE REVENUE
MODELS

Monetisation starts after user acquisition — not before. The core game is free. Revenue comes from enhancing the social experience, not gating it. Daily Joker is never gated — it is the primary retention hook.

🏆
Primary

Premium League Commissioner

Larger leagues (50+ managers), custom league branding, advanced stats, ad-free. One-time tournament upgrade at £4.99–£9.99. Social pressure built-in: if your friend wants a premium league, they see the value immediately.

🤝
Secondary

Sponsored Challenges

Brands sponsor in-game challenges ("Pepsi Golden Boot Challenge"). Brand pays £5K–£50K per campaign; prize funding comes from sponsor. World Cup is one of the most valuable brand activation contexts on Earth.

🎨
At 30K Users

Cosmetic Microtransactions

Kit colours, manager avatars, national team "Fan Packs" (£3.99 per nation). A "Brazil Fan Pack" converts better than a generic upgrade — it connects the purchase to emotional fandom, not transactional utility.

📊
2027 Roadmap

B2B Data & Insights

Aggregated, anonymised behavioural data (captain selections, transfer patterns, Joker usage) sold via API to sports media and betting operators. £500–5K/month. Build the infrastructure now; monetise at scale.

🛡️ What Stays Free — Always

Squad building · Public leagues · Points & standings · Match recaps · Wildcard & Triple Captain chips · Daily Joker (retention hook — never gate this) · Basic in-app chat. The paywall sits on enhancements, never on core gameplay.

// Product Strategy

THREE BETS
WE MUST WIN

These are not features — they are product convictions. Competitors can copy individual mechanics. They cannot copy a fully integrated experience built around these three beliefs.

🃏

Bet 1: Daily Joker is ForzaKit's Signature

No other major platform forces a meaningful tactical decision every single matchday. The Joker creates a daily appointment, a daily risk, and daily bragging rights — the game inside the game.

Must be celebrated in UI, shared socially, never gated

💬

Bet 2: League Chat is Infrastructure

Every other platform treats chat as a feature. ForzaKit treats it as the product. Match-event notifications firing into chat, automated moment cards, weekly summaries that roast or celebrate managers by name.

Each league = a living group chat with points attached

⚽

Bet 3: Pitch View = Share Surface

The squad builder is where a user stops thinking spreadsheet and starts thinking "my team." Every design decision on this screen passes one test: "Would someone screenshot and share this?"

A squad card in WhatsApp = a distribution event

// Brand Strategy

BRAND
IDENTITY

ForzaKit should feel like the brilliant mate who knows football deeply, has strong opinions, and makes the group chat more fun. Not a corporation. Not an official partner. Not a betting site. A fan product — with the visual confidence of a premium brand.

Three Personality Pillars
🧠

Knowledgeable but Not a Bore

Respects fan intelligence. Never condescends, never disappears into jargon. Assumes you know the game; helps you play it better.

🏆

Competitive but Warm

Built around competition — between friends, not strangers with cash. Teasing not cutting. "You paid how much for that defender?" not "optimise your defensive depth rating."

✦

Confident Without Corporate Cringe

Has an aesthetic point of view and owns it. Doesn't apologise for not being FIFA official. Leans into being the alternative.

Updated Taglines
★

Primary: "Your squad. Your tournament."

Ownership, personalisation, World Cup stakes in five words. Works as a headline, campaign line, app store opener. Replaces "Fantasy League" which does zero brand work.

🤝

League Invites: "May the best manager win."

Warm, competitive, slightly ironic. Reads as a challenge to the user's social circle.

🎯

Onboarding: "Kit up. Pick your XI. Make it count."

Instructional energy. Best for how-to-play page and first-run experience.

📊 Competitive Tone Comparison

FPL feels like a spreadsheet with a football theme. FIFA Fantasy feels like a government website. Sorare feels like a fintech app that also likes football. ForzaKit should feel like: an album drop from a football fan who can design.

🔐
CRITICAL PRE-LAUNCH: ROW LEVEL SECURITY
All Supabase tables currently have RLS disabled (Alpha mode). Before any public user touches the app, every table needs RLS policies — users can only read/write their own squads, league membership gating, admin-only writes on fixtures. This is the single biggest security gap.
ARCHITECTURE DECISION
Modular Monolith on Supabase — not microservices. For a 2-person team on a 7-week runway, a monolith is the correct call. PostgREST handles all CRUD automatically. Edge Functions provide serverless escape hatches only where compute is needed (points engine, recap generation). No GraphQL — overkill at this stage.
FIRM POST-MVP DEFERRALS
— Native iOS/Android (React Native + Expo, post-tournament)
— ML squad recommendations ("ForzaKit AI" form-based picks)
— Video highlights (rights-restricted, expensive)
— Auction draft real-time room (complex multiplayer state)
— Prize pool payouts (KYC + gambling license by jurisdiction)
— Multi-tournament UI (schema already supports tournament_id)
